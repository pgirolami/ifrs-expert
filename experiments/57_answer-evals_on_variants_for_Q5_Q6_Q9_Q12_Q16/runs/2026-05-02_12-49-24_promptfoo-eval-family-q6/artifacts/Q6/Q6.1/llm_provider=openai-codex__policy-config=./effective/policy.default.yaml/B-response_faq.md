>Déterminer quels éléments permettent d’établir l’existence du pouvoir sur une entité au sens d’IFRS 10 dans l’analyse du contrôle

Pour cette réponse, on considère que
  - La question est formulée de manière générale et ne décrit pas un cas factuel précis d’investisseur ou d’entité.
  - La réponse vise uniquement les éléments permettant d’établir le pouvoir au sens d’IFRS 10, c’est-à-dire la capacité actuelle de diriger les activités pertinentes de l’entité.
  - Les éléments de pouvoir sont analysés dans le cadre plus large du contrôle IFRS 10, sans traiter ici de manière exhaustive les deux autres critères du contrôle que sont l’exposition aux rendements variables et le lien entre pouvoir et rendements.

**Oui**, Oui, IFRS 10 identifie plusieurs bases permettant d’établir le pouvoir, mais leur application dépend des faits: activités pertinentes, droits substantifs, absence de simples droits protectifs, et capacité actuelle de direction. Il faut donc apprécier chaque voie à la lumière de la structure concrète de l’entité.

Cette voie est pertinente si, dans la situation analysée, les activités pertinentes sont dirigées par vote et si les droits de vote donnent à l’investisseur la capacité actuelle de les diriger. IFRS 10 précise qu’un détenteur de la majorité a en principe le pouvoir, sous réserve que ses droits soient substantifs et qu’aucun autre droit existant ne retire cette capacité (ifrs10 B35, B36). Même sans majorité, le pouvoir peut exister si l’investisseur a la capacité pratique de diriger unilatéralement les activités pertinentes compte tenu de la dispersion des autres porteurs et des autres faits et circonstances (ifrs10 B41, B42, B43, B45)(IFRS 10 B35, IFRS 10 B36, IFRS 10 B41, IFRS 10 B42, IFRS 10 B43, IFRS 10 B45).
Cette approche s’applique lorsque les droits de vote ne sont pas le facteur déterminant ou ne concernent que des tâches administratives, et que des accords contractuels donnent la capacité actuelle de diriger les activités pertinentes. IFRS 10 retient expressément les droits d’appointement, de révocation, de direction ou de veto sur des transactions significatives comme sources possibles de pouvoir (ifrs10 10, B15, B17). L’analyse doit aussi tenir compte de la conception de l’entité et des droits intégrés à sa structure ou à des contrats étroitement liés à ses activités (ifrs10 B51, B52, B53)(IFRS 10 10, IFRS 10 B15, IFRS 10 B17, IFRS 10 B51, IFRS 10 B52, IFRS 10 B53).
Cette voie est applicable si l’investisseur détient des options, instruments convertibles ou forwards qui lui donnent, seuls ou avec d’autres droits, la capacité actuelle de diriger les activités pertinentes. IFRS 10 impose cependant de ne considérer les droits potentiels que s’ils sont substantifs, ce qui suppose notamment une capacité pratique d’exercice au moment où les décisions doivent être prises (ifrs10 B47, B50, B23, B24). L’analyse inclut aussi l’objet et les caractéristiques de l’instrument et de l’implication globale de l’investisseur (ifrs10 B48, B49)(IFRS 10 B47, IFRS 10 B48, IFRS 10 B49, IFRS 10 B50, IFRS 10 B23, IFRS 10 B24).
Cette approche est pertinente lorsque le pouvoir de décision a été délégué à un gestionnaire ou autre décideur. IFRS 10 exige alors d’apprécier si ce décideur agit comme principal ou comme agent; seul un principal peut contrôler l’entité via ses droits décisionnels (ifrs10 B58, B60, B61). L’évaluation repose sur l’étendue du pouvoir discrétionnaire, les droits détenus par d’autres parties, la rémunération et l’exposition du décideur à la variabilité des rendements (ifrs10 B62, B64, B65, B71, B72)(IFRS 10 B58, IFRS 10 B60, IFRS 10 B61, IFRS 10 B62, IFRS 10 B64, IFRS 10 B65, IFRS 10 B71, IFRS 10 B72).
Cette approche complète l’analyse lorsque les droits formels ne suffisent pas, à eux seuls, à rendre la conclusion évidente. IFRS 10 demande alors d’examiner les indices de capacité pratique à diriger unilatéralement l’entité et les relations spéciales avec l’investee, tout en donnant davantage de poids aux preuves directes de pouvoir qu’aux simples indicateurs (ifrs10 B18 à B21). L’investisseur doit aussi tenir compte des droits et expositions détenus via des agents de fait, c’est-à-dire des parties qu’il peut diriger pour agir en son nom (ifrs10 B73, B74, B75)(IFRS 10 B18, IFRS 10 B19, IFRS 10 B20, IFRS 10 B21, IFRS 10 B73, IFRS 10 B74, IFRS 10 B75).
Cette approche n’est pertinente que si la question porte sur une portion d’entité dont les actifs, passifs et flux sont, en substance, cantonnés et isolés du reste de l’investee. IFRS 10 exige alors d’apprécier séparément le pouvoir sur ce silo, en identifiant les activités qui affectent significativement ses rendements et la manière dont elles sont dirigées (ifrs10 B76 à B79). En l’absence d’un tel cantonnement économique et juridique, cette voie ne s’applique pas(IFRS 10 B76, IFRS 10 B77, IFRS 10 B78, IFRS 10 B79).

    >An investor that holds more than half of the voting rights of an investee has power in the following situations, unless paragraph B36 or paragraph B37 applies: **(a)the relevant activities are directed by a vote of the holder of the majority of the voting rights, or (b)a majority of the members of the governing body that directs the relevant activities are appointed by a vote of the holder of the majority of the voting rights.**

 - [IFRS 10 B36](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B36)

    >For an investor that holds more than half of the voting rights of an investee, to have power over an investee, **the investor’s voting rights must be substantive, in accordance with paragraphs B22⁠–⁠B25, and must provide the investor with the current ability to direct the relevant activities**, which often will be through determining operating and financing policies. **If another entity has existing rights that provide that entity with the right to direct the relevant activities and that entity is not an agent of the investor, the investor does not have power over the investee.**

 - [IFRS 10 B41](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B41)

    >When assessing whether an investor’s voting rights are sufficient to give it power, an investor considers all facts and circumstances, including: **(a)the size of the investor’s holding of voting rights relative to the size and dispersion of holdings of the other vote holders** ... **(b)potential voting rights held by the investor, other vote holders or other parties** ... **(c)rights arising from other contractual arrangements** ... and **(d)any additional facts and circumstances that indicate the investor has, or does not have, the current ability to direct the relevant activities at the time that decisions need to be made**, including voting patterns at previous shareholders’ meetings.

 - [IFRS 10 B43](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B43)

    >When the direction of relevant activities is determined by majority vote and **an investor holds significantly more voting rights than any other vote holder or organised group of vote holders, and the other shareholdings are widely dispersed**, it may be clear, after considering the factors listed in paragraph B42(a)⁠–⁠(c) alone, that **the investor has power over the investee.**

 - [IFRS 10 B45](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B45)

    >However, the factors listed in paragraph B42(a)⁠–⁠(c) alone may not be conclusive. If an investor, having considered those factors, is unclear whether it has power, **it shall consider additional facts and circumstances, such as whether other shareholders are passive in nature as demonstrated by voting patterns at previous shareholders’ meetings.** This includes the assessment of the factors set out in paragraph B18 and the indicators in paragraphs B19 and B20.

 - [IFRS 10 10](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_10)

    >An investor has power over an investee when **the investor has existing rights that give it the current ability to direct the relevant activities**, ie the activities that significantly affect the investee’s returns.

 - [IFRS 10 B15](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B15)

    >Examples of rights that, either individually or in combination, can give an investor power include but are not limited to: **(b)rights to appoint, reassign or remove members of an investee’s key management personnel who have the ability to direct the relevant activities ; (c)rights to appoint or remove another entity that directs the relevant activities; (d)rights to direct the investee to enter into, or veto any changes to, transactions for the benefit of the investor; and (e)other rights (such as decision‑making rights specified in a management contract) that give the holder the ability to direct the relevant activities.**

 - [IFRS 10 B17](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B17)

    >When voting rights cannot have a significant effect on an investee’s returns, such as when voting rights relate to administrative tasks only and **contractual arrangements determine the direction of the relevant activities**, the investor needs to assess those contractual arrangements in order to determine whether it has rights sufficient to give it power over the investee.

 - [IFRS 10 B51](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B51)

    >In assessing the purpose and design of an investee (see paragraphs B5⁠–⁠B8), an investor shall consider the involvement and decisions made at the investee’s inception as part of its design and evaluate whether **the transaction terms and features of the involvement provide the investor with rights that are sufficient to give it power.**

 - [IFRS 10 B52](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B52)

    >In addition, an investor shall consider contractual arrangements such as call rights, put rights and liquidation rights established at the investee’s inception. **When these contractual arrangements involve activities that are closely related to the investee, then these activities are, in substance, an integral part of the investee’s overall activities** ... **decision‑making rights embedded in contractual arrangements that are closely related to the investee need to be considered as relevant activities when determining power over the investee.**

 - [IFRS 10 B53](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B53)

    >For some investees, relevant activities occur only when particular circumstances arise or events occur. The investee may be designed so that the direction of its activities and its returns are predetermined unless and until those particular circumstances arise or events occur. In this case, **only the decisions about the investee’s activities when those circumstances or events occur can significantly affect its returns and thus be relevant activities.** The circumstances or events need not have occurred for an investor with the ability to make those decisions to have power.

 - [IFRS 10 B47](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B47)

    >When assessing control, an investor considers its potential voting rights as well as potential voting rights held by other parties, to determine whether it has power. Potential voting rights are rights to obtain voting rights of an investee, such as those arising from convertible instruments or options, including forward contracts. **Those potential voting rights are considered only if the rights are substantive** (see paragraphs B22⁠–⁠B25).

 - [IFRS 10 B48](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B48)

    >When considering potential voting rights, an investor shall consider **the purpose and design of the instrument, as well as the purpose and design of any other involvement the investor has with the investee.** This includes an assessment of the various terms and conditions of the instrument as well as the investor’s apparent expectations, motives and reasons for agreeing to those terms and conditions.

 - [IFRS 10 B49](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B49)

    >If the investor also has voting or other decision‑making rights relating to the investee’s activities, **the investor assesses whether those rights, in combination with potential voting rights, give the investor power.**

 - [IFRS 10 B50](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B50)

    >**Substantive potential voting rights alone, or in combination with other rights, can give an investor the current ability to direct the relevant activities.**

 - [IFRS 10 B58](https://www.ifrs.org/issued-standards/list-of-standards/ifrs10/content/dam/ifrs/publications/html-standards/english/2026/issued/ifrs10/#/IFRS10_B58)
