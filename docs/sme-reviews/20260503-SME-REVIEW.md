## Further discussion about SME's workflow

SME doesn't see the benefit of the assistant because the answers to all these questions are in NAVIS.

Most of the work is not so much diving into the standards to extract information from them because 
- there are only a few and are known after a few years
- interpretative material provided by E&Y, PwC or Lefebvre are much more operational are usable

The workflow of IFRS experts is typically to use all this material, find relevant information within the company and make a determination. This requires reading through documents (contracts,...), understanding corporate structures, discussing precise items with stakeholders across the company (users of software, factory directors, procurement specialists, etc).

Consequently, the assistant in its current form is useful only as a stepping stone towards a more autonomous system that would also be able to go through the documentation the expert currently studies and reason on them + standards/interpretative documents
